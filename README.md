# AI Biotech Research Triage Workflow

An AI-assisted prototype for turning scientific paper abstracts into clear, structured research notes.

## The problem

Keeping up with biological research can be time-consuming. Important information is often buried in technical abstracts, and papers may need to be reviewed before someone can decide whether they are relevant.

I created this workflow to explore how AI could support the first stage of research review without replacing human judgement.

## How it works

The user provides the title and abstract of a scientific paper. The workflow then:

1. Summarises the research in plain English.
2. Identifies the biological problem being studied.
3. Extracts the methods and main findings.
4. Explains its possible relevance to drug discovery.
5. Highlights limitations and unanswered questions.
6. Organises the information into a consistent format.

The output must be checked against the original abstract before being used.

## Why I chose this project

My degree combined biotechnology with enterprise, and I am interested in how AI can make scientific work more efficient. This project allowed me to combine my understanding of biology with my interest in practical AI workflows.

## Tools

* ChatGPT
* Structured prompt instructions
* Publicly available scientific abstracts
* GitHub for documentation

## Limitations

The workflow only analyses the information provided to it. It cannot verify experimental results, access missing details from a full paper or determine whether a study is scientifically reliable.

## Next steps

I plan to test the workflow on different areas of biological research, compare its output with the original abstracts and explore how the results could be organised automatically in Google Sheets or Notion.
